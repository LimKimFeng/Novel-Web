Chapter 60: S-Rank!

[You have used 10 Premium Gacha Tickets]

[Spinning...]

As soon as the first notification appeared, Ethan held his breath and covered his eyes with his hands, as an intense golden light flooded the office and momentarily blinded him.

It clearly wasn’t an S-Rank item, but it was still amazing!

[Congratulations, you have received: 1x Random Character Selection Card (Rank A)]

[Item: Random Character Selection Card

Description: An item that allows you to generate a random character of a specific profession. This character will have a Rank A talent, as well as unique skills for their profession.

Rank: A]

Ethan’s eyes sparkled so brightly that he barely paid attention to the other items, already imagining what kind of character he might summon.

There were so many professions essential to the basic functioning of a society and that simply didn’t exist in Goldenveil.

That reward wasn’t Rank S, but it was just as wonderful!

[Congratulations! You have received: 1x Subordinate Talent Enhancement Potion! (Rank B)]

[Congratulations! You have received: 1x Three-Step Poison Vial (Rank C)]

[Congratulations! You have received: 1x 50kg of Lunar Green Grass Spirit Seeds (Rank D)]

[Congratulations! You have received: 1x Random Mana Skill Book (Rank C)]

[Congratulations! You have received: 1x High-Quality Weapon Kit (Rank D)]

[Congratulations! You have received: 1x Underground Prison Blueprint (Rank D)]

[Congratulations! You have received: 1x Dawn Blade Dagger (Rank B)]

[Congratulations! You have received: 1x Recipe for Rising Red Flower Wine (Rank C)]

Right after the ninth item, which Ethan had greatly appreciated, he wasn’t expecting anything else good and was already ready to focus on his Rank A item.

But he soon noticed something: the pause between spins was much longer than before.

The floor beneath his feet was shaken by a gentle tremor, and a golden light appeared, much more intense than the one that had accompanied the Rank A item.

It was as if a second sun had risen inside the office.

When the light faded and Ethan’s vision cleared, he could barely contain his excitement.

An S-Rank item, finally!

[Congratulations! You have received: 1x Supreme Mana Beast Egg (S-Rank)]

[Name: Supreme Mana Beast Egg

Description: The egg of a high-level mana beast, accompanied by formations that will immediately connect it to the host during hatching.

The beast’s race is unknown, and it will hatch in one year and four months. Rank: S]

"A mana beast..." Ethan’s lips curled up so high they seemed about to reach his ears.

It was a mana beast, and one that would be tamed from birth, to boot.

’System... what are the odds of it being a dragon?’

[A dragon’s rank wouldn’t be lower than SSS]

"Damn..." Ethan cursed under his breath, defeated.

He wanted a dragon, he wanted a dragon so fucking bad! A dragon that could take human form and had huge tits!

What kind of man would he be if he lived his whole life without even a single dragon mommy to call his own?

What would he have to do? Marry an ugly woman and name her his dragon?

Well... that would be stupid and unnecessarily cruel.

There was no way that beast would be born as some kind of stupid worm that would only serve as a pet, right?

Nah... no way.

Life wouldn’t be that cruel to him.

In the end, even though that beast was still many years away from becoming useful, Ethan didn’t care.

It was an S-Rank item. His first S-Rank item.

It wasn’t as good as it would be if it were a unique S-Rank heroine, but it was still very good.

Besides, Ethan also discovered that there were items beyond S-Rank, going all the way up to SSS-Rank.

It wasn’t something important to know, but it was still interesting.

With a sigh, he focused on the other three rewards that interested him.

A Rank B Talent Enhancement Potion, which he would give to Evelyn. A blueprint for an underground prison project, which would be important for Goldenveil’s future. And, of course, the character card.

The latter, though not comparable to an S-Rank item, was still what Ethan wanted most.

The reason was simple.

Goldenveil didn’t have a blacksmith, nor anyone capable of working with metal.

The guards knew a little about maintaining and repairing their own weapons, but that was the limit, and weapons weren’t all they needed.

Ethan’s horse, for example, which was being cared for by a local hunter, already needed new shoes, and the tools the system provided simply weren’t enough for the entire population of Goldenveil.

They already had a forge ready, right in the old blacksmith’s house.

It was a fully equipped forge, built by the Darkwall family, who planned to make the settlement as self-sufficient as possible.

It had been abandoned for a long time and would finally have a new owner.

With a quick motion, Ethan tore up the letter, immediately greeted by a system screen with hundreds of professions to choose from.

After scrolling through the screen a bit, he finally found what he wanted: blacksmith.

Without hesitation, he clicked.

[Do you wish to summon a random character with Rank A talent and the blacksmith profession?

The character will be a veteran in the role, aged between thirty and fifty, and at the second stage.]

Ethan didn’t hesitate to click yes.

The moment he selected the option, the torn letter shifted, transforming into hundreds of tiny fragments of light that gathered before him, forming a male figure.

If Ethan had a way to describe him, it would simply be: rough.

He was tall, even taller than Ethan himself.

He was burly, but most of that bulk was muscle rather than fat, dressed in brown pants, a white shirt, and a gray-green apron.

The man’s eyes, dark and serene, were seized by a momentary shock.

His whole body shuddered, and vomit seemed to rise in his throat.

He held it back, staring at the younh man in front of him, a ton of information flooding his mind all at once.

It was hard to process so much happening so fast.
