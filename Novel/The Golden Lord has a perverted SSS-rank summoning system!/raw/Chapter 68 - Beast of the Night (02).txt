The burial of the four bodies did not take long.

One of the elders said a brief prayer, and the adventurers were laid to rest in the Goldenveil cemetery, along with all their belongings.

There was even one item that caught Ethan’s eye: a Rank B sword belonging to the young man who had led the group, but he suppressed his own greed.

He was an honorable Lord, not a cheap bandit.

The sword was buried alongside the young man, and after everything was finished, Ethan ordered Doran to gather the hunters at the barracks, then went to see his wives.

He already had a plan to deal with the wolf, and with Alice’s help, that plan would be much easier to carry out.

The beast was intelligent, but it was still an animal, and in the end, there are few things predators love more than easy prey, just as there are few things they fear more than flames.

With a broad smile, Ethan returned to the mansion.

"Oh..." Evelyn’s lips parted in understanding, while something heavy settled in Alice’s chest.

"Are you going to put yourself in danger again?" the golden-haired beauty asked, drawing a sigh from Ethan.

The space was about seven hundred square meters, more than enough for a test.

"Do you have control over the shape?" he asked.

Honestly, he hadn’t even considered the possibility of Alice failing.

She was an S-Rank talent, and if she focused her mind, she could probably use that ability with sheer willpower and raw mana alone, without needing any formal knowledge.

That was the talent of an S-Rank. They had superior minds and bodies, fate itself bowing before every step they took. Failure, in the end, was for the talentless.

"Hm... I think so. What do you want me to do?"

"Something like a box, with walls of fire and a single opening."

Alice was confused for a moment, but she thought briefly and nodded, her beautiful face set with determination.

Her hands opened, palms facing the ground.

Immediately, the mana around her shuddered, her colossal mana reserves surging like a tide.

"Burn..." Her voice echoed, sweet and serene, yet carrying an intent that made her feel like a goddess of war given form.

In the next instant, the world around her burst into flames.

Walls of golden fire rose gloriously, sealing off the entire space except for the spot where she stood, leaving only an opening of about two meters.

The heat pulsed against Ethan’s skin, and he found himself gasping involuntarily.

Alice was, honestly, incredible.

With the proper training and without her naivety she could easily become a war machine capable of wiping out entire armies on her own.

Of course, he would never force such a fate upon her. He wanted his sweet, naive, gentle Alice, not some cold goddess of war.

Silently, Alice nodded and walked alongside Evelyn, accompanying Their Man.

Doran had acted quickly, gathering all the hunters and city guards. Work outside the wall had been halted, and an order had been issued: no one was to leave until further notice from the Lord.

That heightened anxiety among the citizens, but in the end, everyone waited in silence.

Whatever their Lord decided, they were certain it would be the best choice for them.
