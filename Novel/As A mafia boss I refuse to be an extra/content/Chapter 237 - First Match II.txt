Chapter 237: First Match II

But Zavier didn’t stop.

His mind screamed the same word over and over.

’Die die die die die die–’

The referee physically grabbed him, using S- rank strength to pull him off.

Only then did Zavier stop.

He stood slowly, ran his bloody hands across his face, smearing the crimson, brought them to his nose.

And inhaled deeply!

Then he smiled – a creepy, unsettling smile that made Nobles across the stadium feel chills.

"Ah... the smell of disgusting Nobles."

"!!!"

The words carried across the silent arena, amplified by the stadium’s audio system.

BOOM!

The stadium exploded into cheerful roars that shook the barriers, commoners screaming themselves hoarse, the noise becoming physical pressure.

"STORMHOLD! STORMHOLD! STORMHOLD!"

That Zavier fellow though... that level of violence at the end was extreme. The referee had to physically restrain him. That creepy smile! And those words... there is clearly a story behind this!"

[Tranquil City - Viewing Room]

Gerard jumped to his feet, his massive frame shaking the floor, his first pumping the air.

"THIS IS IT! OUR CHILDREN ARE MONSTERS TOO NOW! THEY’RE JUST AS STRONG AS ANY NOBLE! DID YOU SEE THAT?!"

SMACK!

Mira’s hand connected with his head again, harder this time.

"STOP CALLING EVERYONE MONSTERS!"

But she was smiling, tears in her eyes, pride overwhelming her irritation.

Marcus and Elaine Morwen held each other, crying openly.

"Are those really our kids? They’re so strong and so capable. I can’t believe–"

Takeshi and Yuki sat with perfect composure, but their hands were clasped tightly, their eyes showing pride they couldn’t quite hide.

Sakura was staring at the screen, her eyes wide.

"They were amazing. All of them. But especially..."

She looked at Damian’s figure standing outside the arena, completely calm, like he’d expected exactly this outcome.

James Kael spoke quietly, his rough voice cutting through the celebration.

"I’m more worried about that Zavier kid. He lost his family only recently... look at him."

Everyone turned to the screen showing Zavier’s creepy smile, the blood on his face, the emptiness in his eyes.

"..."

Silence spread across the room.

Yuki spoke softly, her voice carrying motherly concern.

"We should give him some love as well. Invite him to family dinners. Show him he’s not alone. He’s just a kid who’s been through something no child should experience."

Heads nodded around the room, the celebration tempered by awareness of what violence cost.

[Arena One - Outside the Barrier]

Edrin and the others walked out of the arena, medical teams rushing past them to treat the defeated opponents.

Adrian stood with his mouth hanging open, his silly demeanor completely gone, replaced by genuine shock.

’What the fuck! Are all of them monsters now?! Five seconds! They won in FIVE SECONDS!’

Victoria and Fiona looked at Zavier with obvious wariness, stepping back slightly as he approached.

But Zavier just smiled at them – that same creepy smile.

"Don’t worry. I don’t eat all Nobles. Only the ones against us."

The reassurance somehow made it worse.

Seraphina and Elizabeth exchanged meaningful glances, both thinking the same thing.

’Headmaster told us about Damian’s training methods. But this is insane. These aren’t students anymore.’

The enemy Academy’s professor arrived, his face red with fury.

"Why were you so cruel with my students?! That level of violence was completely unnecessary! They’re going to be traumatized!"

He was a Noble too, his indignation carrying the weight of class offense.

Damian ignored him completely, turning to his team with a genuine smile.

"Good job. Even I’m surprised by how effortless that was. You’ve all grown incredibly strong."

His Mafia members practically glowed at the praise, their exhaustion disappearing.

"Let’s go back. No point watching the other matches. They’ll be slower and less interesting."

Under his lead, the Stormhold team began walking back toward their preparation area.

And as they walked, Damian felt something.

Attention... Focused and intense attention.

He turned his head slightly, looking toward Arena Two where Imperial Academy had just finished their own match.

Victor Cross stood there, his face pale, his hands clenched into fists so tight they shook.

Their eyes met across the distance.

Damian’s smile became fierce – predatory, challenging and absolutely confident.

Then he looked away, dismissing Victor entirely, continuing to walk with his team.

Victor stood frozen, rage and fear mixing, unable to look away from Damian’s retreating back.

’Five seconds. They won in five seconds. And he wasn’t even fighting.

What happens when he actually enters the arena?’

The thought made his blood run cold.
