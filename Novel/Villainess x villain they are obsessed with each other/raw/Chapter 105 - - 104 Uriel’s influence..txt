Chapter 105 -: 104 Uriel’s influence.

"Are you sure you want to go?" Julius asked, sitting beside Rosalina inside the carriage.

They were heading to the academy, but Julius still looked a little worried after everything that happened yesterday.

"You should rest. If the avatar wasn’t coming today, I wouldn’t have gone either."

Rosalina smiled and locked her arm with his.

She leaned her head comfortably on his shoulder.

"Don’t worry, my body heals super fast. See?" She moved her face closer to him.

Julius quickly leaned back in surprise.

He stared at her cheek, but there was no mark or bruise left at all.

Rosalina giggled when she saw his red face.

"Why are you getting shy all of a sudden?" she asked playfully.

"Weren’t you the one always shouting embarrassing things? ’My Rose this, my Rose that’~"

Julius scratched his cheek awkwardly and laughed a little.

"Yeah... but I didn’t think it would happen so fast. And knowing you’ve loved me for such a long time... it was a big surprise."

He paused, then added with a shy grin, "Most importantly, I didn’t expect you to become this clingy after confessing. Yesterday you were hugging me like a koala!"

Rosalina laughed softly and hugged his arm even tighter. "Well, too bad. You’re stuck with clingy me now."

Julius shook his head with a smile, then turned a bit more serious.

"Anyway... what do you think? Do you have any ideas to deal with that stupid system?"

After waking up, he had already told her almost everything.

He said he was a regressor (he didn’t mention he was actually a transmigrator yet, that felt too shocking for now).

He told her about the protagonist and all the dangerous powers the system gave him.

Rosalina frowned a little when she heard the question.

"Now that you mention it... it’s really troublesome. I never thought something so powerful could exist."

She rubbed her head.

"He can go back in time, become super strong, and even make people like him... It won’t be easy to fight someone like that."

She thought for a moment, then asked, "By the way, Nickel also has a system, right?"

"Yeah," Julius nodded. "But he said his is much weaker. It can’t do time regression or those crazy overpowered things."

"I guess it makes sense. Changing the future twice would probably break everything and cause a lot of problems."

Julius let out a small sigh and looked a bit worried again.

"Don’t make that face," Rosalina said gently. She reached up and softly rubbed his cheek.

"We’ll find a way together." She smiled encouragingly.

"In the past life you told me about, he couldn’t kill you, right?"

"Yeah," Julius nodded. He had also told her how she died in that life.

Surprisingly, Instead of getting angry, Rosalina had comforted him, which made Julius feel very grateful.

"Are there other transmigrators like Nickel?" she asked.

"I asked him, but he said he doesn’t know. We can start searching for more now. Since the main story has started according to Nickel, more things should start showing up soon."

Julius stayed quiet for a moment.

Since Nickel’s case, he realized anything could happen.

Schemes could come from anywhere. So he decided he must give the divination board to his avatar as soon as possible.

That way, if something bad happened again like last time, his avatar could use the board to find out the real reason behind it.

After all, his avatar was the Saint.

With the divination board, the Saint’s powers would become much stronger.

"What’s next?" Rosalina asked, gently breaking his thoughts.

"You said the famous Lone King’s inheritance would appear soon. Are you going to join it?"

"Yeah..." Julius nodded softly.

"I really need the time-path inheritance inside it."

He paused and looked straight ahead. A cold glint flashed in his eyes.

"And... it’s about time to check the hero’s regression abilities."

He needed to see exactly how far the protagonist could go back in time.

According to the novel, the hero would always return to a "safe point."

For example, if he regressed right now, he would probably go back only to yesterday.

Julius also wanted to know how strong the hero really was and how many skills he had.

As they were talking, the carriage slowed down and stopped.

Alfred opened the door right away. "We are here, my lord," he said politely, holding the door wide open.

Julius and Rosalina gave him a small nod and stepped out.

The moment they got down, their eyes fell on the large gate of the academy.

"They’re really going all out, aren’t they?"

Rosalina muttered, staring at the beautiful decorations with wide eyes.

Julius let out a soft chuckle. "Well, he is the Saint now."

The gate looked amazing.

It was covered in fresh flowers, shining golden symbols of the Church of Benigore, and even white ribbons fluttering in the breeze.

After all, the Saint was an even bigger holy symbol than the Pope herself.

It made sense they would welcome him like this.

"I wonder what their faces will look like when they realize it’s you," Rosalina said with a playful laugh.

She locked her arm with his again and gave it a little squeeze.

"The great and holy Saint was just an avatar of a lovesick fool..."

"Hey, that was private!" Julius whispered back, his ears turning pink.

He tried to look serious but failed and ended up grinning.

"They might even faint from shock. Or start praying on the spot thinking I’ve been replaced by a clone."

Rosalina giggled and leaned closer. "If they faint, I’ll catch you and run away. My strong Saint needs protecting from his own fans."

"Deal," Julius joked as he took a step forward and walked through the grand gate with Rosalina by his side.

They entered through the grand gate, and the moment they stepped inside, the full view hit their eyes.

The entire academy grounds were beautifully decorated like a festival.

Colorful flowers lined every path, white and gold banners fluttered in the breeze, and lanterns hung from the trees even though it was daytime.

In the middle of the grounds stood a grand platform, and right on top of it was a large, shining statue of the goddess Benigore.

The statue looked so real that it almost felt like she was watching everyone with a kind smile.

Julius looked around with his mouth slightly open in shock.

Students were running here and there, working hard to make everything perfect.

Some were fixing flowers, others were polishing the platform, and a group of girls were carefully placing white roses around the goddess statue.

Everyone looked excited and nervous at the same time.

"I didn’t think a saint would have this much influence," Julius muttered, still staring at the busy scene. "This is way more than I expected..."
