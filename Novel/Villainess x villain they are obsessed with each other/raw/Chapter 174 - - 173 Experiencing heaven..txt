The dean just stood there, staring straight ahead. His face twisted with shock, eyes wide and mouth hanging open. The sheer absurdity of the sight hit him like a hammer.

"What kind of absurd joke is this...?" he muttered. His legs gave out, and he slowly sank to his knees on the broken floor.

What he saw was a massive space tear still hanging in the air. Lava dripped from its jagged edges, glowing bright red and orange.

The tear had not closed at all. Heat rolled off it in waves, making the air shimmer and sting his skin.

The ground around it stayed scorched and cracked, as if the force that ripped the space open was too strong for the tower to heal.

He stayed on his knees, heart pounding hard in his chest.

A storm of feelings crashed through him, confusion, fear, and deep awe. He had been so sure the masked figures were Julius and Rosalina.

He had come here ready to kill them. But now everything looked different. Far more terrifying.

These traces left behind were not normal.

’The one who fought here...’ he turned his head slowly toward the space tear. That person was someone the dean could not even begin to understand.

From the looks of it, the tear had been made with extreme speed and raw power. Clear signs of fire path energy still burned in the air, thick and heavy.

His earlier guess that the caster was at Void Core stage had been completely wrong.

Something like this could only be done by someone at the Infinite Core stage, the dean realized.

He swallowed hard, his messy beard brushing against his chest as he breathed. Right now, he did not know if he was lucky that he had not arrived earlier... or unlucky that he had entered the tower at all.

If either of those spell casters had still been here, he would have died without any doubt.

The dean stayed on his knees like that for what felt like an hour or two. His mind had gone completely blank.

Everything he once believed had vanished like mist under the morning sun. He sat there, utterly dumbfounded by how differently things had turned out.

"I better get out of here," he finally decided.

There was no one around right now, but who knew if the ones who fought here might return? It was a million times better to be safe than sorry.

He pushed himself up quickly, turned around, and walked back the way he came.

Since he had not taken the trials on the tenth or eleventh floors, no barriers stopped him. He willed himself out of the tower and left without any trouble.
