The sounds grew worse, raw agony mixed with the wet rip of something tearing.

It sounded like Rizen was having the worst night of his life. The woman let out a soft, amused chuckle as she kept walking.

She reached up and slowly pulled off her mask. Her face came into view, almost identical to Elizabeth’s, except her eyes were a bright, lively green instead of red.

Long blonde hair tumbled down to her waist. This was Kathlyin Kazier, twin sister of the Hayes clan matriarch and leader of the Red Unit under the patriarch’s direct command.

Still smiling to herself, she walked to a quiet corner a little way from the noisy screams.

She took out a crystal orb the size of a football and set it gently on the floor. After a quick glance around, she pulled out a small mirror and a makeup box.

Without any rush, she fixed her tangled hair, touched up her face, and made everything neat again.

Once she looked perfect, she reached out with one hand and drew a bit of her compressed mana. The orb lit up with a warm glow. A calm, controlled man’s voice came through.

[Has everything gone according to plan?]

The moment Nathaniel’s voice reached her, a soft blush spread across Kathlyin’s cheeks.

She bent down so her face was level with the glowing orb. Nathaniel’s face appeared inside it.

"Yes," she answered, her voice warm.

[Make sure you don’t leave any evidence there. Use the spells I taught you to kill him and leave his body behind.]

Kathlyin stared at his face in the glowing orb and gave a small nod. "I will do so," she said. "But do you remember the promise you made?"

Nathaniel’s face froze right away. He stared back at her without a word.

The air around her grew cold and still. Her eyes slowly curved into a pretty crescent-moon smile, and her lips did the same.

It was exactly like Elizabeth’s smile, sweet, bright, and full of meaning.

["Well..."] Nathaniel started. His eyes drifted to the side.

"Well?" she asked, still smiling, clearly waiting for the answer she wanted.

["Ahem."] Nathaniel coughed, looking awkward. He knew this woman was just like his Eli.

Same face, same nature. He understood exactly what she wanted. But if he agreed, Elizabeth would beat him later. If he didn’t...

["Very well... I will teach you the secret spell you wanted so badly,"] he said. His voice sounded a little stiff.

"Hmm?" Kathlyin frowned at once. She raised one eyebrow. "Was that the agreement? Was it not..."

Before she could finish, Nathaniel’s voice cut in, panicked. "Ok, see you soon!"

The connection ended with a soft click. The orb went dark and blank.

Kathlyin kept smiling, eyes still curved like little moons, lips still lifted. But a thick vein suddenly popped out on her forehead.

Bam!

She kicked the orb like a football. It flew across the hallway and smashed into pieces against the wall.

"Fucking asshole!" she cursed. Her face turned bright red with sudden anger.

All the happy excitement she had felt vanished in an instant, like morning mist under the sun.

"Damn fuck!" she growled through gritted teeth. She looked very annoyed...even after doing so much and trying her best she wasn’t getting what she wanted.... It was really annoying for her...

The screams from the room had stopped. That meant the big guy was done.

Kathlyin took a slow breath, she controlled her anger perfectly then turned around, and walked back toward the door.

He sat in his working cabin, surrounded by nothing but piles of paper. Stacks covered the desk, the chairs, even parts of the floor.

It looked like a storm of documents had exploded in the room.

"What a predicament..." he muttered, rubbing his face.

He looked completely worn out. His love life had been a complete mess for the last twenty years, and today was no different.
