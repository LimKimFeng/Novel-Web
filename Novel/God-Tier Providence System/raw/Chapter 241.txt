Therefore, Nova, who hailed from a prestigious family, had securely held the position of Empress for many years.

After Aurelia’s father passed away, she naturally inherited the position of Former Empress.

In the original novel, Nova was one of the major mid-game bosses Knox had to face.

This old woman not only possessed formidable cultivation, but her political maneuvering was extraordinary, her scheming venomous, her shrewdness incredibly deep.

Albert’s rebellion, in a certain sense, was entirely due to her constant instigation and encouragement.

She hoped her biological son would sit on the great throne while she herself ruled from behind the curtain.

After Albert was defeated and died, and Aurelia also died of severe illness, Nova immediately came up with a countermeasure.

She directly pushed her youngest son, Varen, onto the imperial throne to stabilize the situation.

In that chaotic scenario, whether to stabilize the people’s hearts or continue holding power, her approach was absolutely flawless.

But unfortunately, such a shrewd woman just had to run into Knox. Subjected to all sorts of bizarre, showy operations, she was directly hit with a plot-induced intelligence debuff, blurting out her own ambitions and plans herself.
