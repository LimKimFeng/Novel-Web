Chapter 117

"Heh, brat, set your mind at ease." Rorin narrowed his eyes and sneered coldly.

"Senior Brother Dariel has always sought revenge for the smallest of grievances. He has never forgiven anyone who humiliated him."

"Didn’t he already arrange for that brat named Taryn to go to the Hidden Broken Sword Cave?"

"Within half a year, he will absolutely die without a doubt. You have no need to overthink it."

"Oh?" Lirin looked curious. "Master, what does that mean?"

"The Hidden Broken Sword Cave is the most terrifying, panic-inducing forbidden area in the entire Divine Blade Sect. Usually, only disciples who have committed grave, unforgivable mistakes are dispatched there for solitary confinement to reflect on their sins." Rorin stroked his chin. A cold sneer on his face.

"Sealed within the Hidden Broken Sword Cave are numerous taboo, ominous swords. Over the years, the entire cave has been completely corroded by Yin energy and baleful aura."

"In the past, disciples locked in solitary confinement there would inevitably have their minds eroded by the baleful aura within a month or two at the shortest, or half a year at the longest. They would go completely mad and draw their swords to commit suicide."

"With that brat’s abilities, if he can last three months, it will be considered a stroke of incredible luck!"

A look of sheer joy finally appeared on Lirin’s face.

"Master, if that’s the case, then that brat truly is dead without a doubt!"

"I already can’t wait to see the miserable look on his face when he dies!"

"Hahahaha..."

...

After the three of them selected their respective masters, they left Feather Transformation Peak.

The Divine Blade Sect had three hundred Outer Sect peaks and thirty Inner Sect peaks. Among the thirty Inner Sect peaks were Ten Great Main Peaks, respectively owned by the Ten Elders.

Aya followed Nerin to Falling Dust Peak and Lirin followed Rorin to Heaven-Turning Peak.

As for Alex, he was taken by Loris, leaving behind the prosperous Ten Great Main Peaks. Heading toward a remote and desolate mountain peak to the north.

Standing on the flying sword and looking down at the overlapping mountain ranges brimming with sword intent, Alex stood with his hands behind his back. "Elder Loris, what kind of place is this Hidden Broken Sword Cave?"

"Could it be that just because I didn’t offer a bribe to Elder Dariel, he wants to banish me to a remote land to fend for myself and die?"

Loris’s expression was complicated and full of frustration.

"You look like a smart kid, how could you do something so foolish?!"

"Senior Brother Dariel is the most narrow-minded and petty person among the Ten Elders. You offended him the moment you entered the sect. How can you possibly have good days ahead?"

"Furthermore, your Lark family had already spent quite a lot beforehand. You were only short the final hundred thousand spirit stones as a meeting gift."

"To fail on the verge of success... falling short at the final hurdle, ah..."

Alex sneered coldly. "I had already spent so many benefits, yet merely because of a hundred thousand spirit stones, I was treated like this by him."

"With Dariel being so boundlessly greedy, disregarding all prior goodwill... even if I gave him a hundred thousand today, there would be another demand for a hundred thousand tomorrow."

"Today we cede five cities, tomorrow we cede ten cities, just to gain a single night of peaceful sleep. But upon waking and looking at the borders, the enemy army has arrived once again."

"Throwing spirit stones into this kind of bottomless pit, when will it ever be filled?"

Loris was utterly dumbfounded. Looking at Alex with an incredibly bizarre expression.

Alex realized he had spoken out of turn. He hurriedly coughed heavily, trying his best to put on an innocent, ignorant posture.

"Elder Loris, you still haven’t told me. What exactly is this Hidden Broken Sword Cave?"

"And who is this Senior Brother Sylra that Elder Dariel mentioned?"

"The Hidden Broken Sword Cave is located on Broken Sword Peak at the northernmost edge of the Divine Blade Sect." Master Loris said profoundly.

"Back in the day, Broken Sword Peak was also one of the Ten Great Main Peaks."

"It’s just that several decades ago, an unforeseen incident occurred within the sect, so it fell into decline."

"As for Senior Brother Sylra, he is just like me. A fellow member of the Ten Elders."

"However, whether you can learn any skills from him will depend entirely on your own fortune."

While speaking, Loris steered his floating cloud, bringing Alex to the airspace above a certain mountain peak.

Not only was the terrain incredibly remote, but it also looked exceptionally gloomy and eerie.

The mountainside was shrouded in thick, pitch-black fog, radiating a sky-piercing aura of Yin energy. Faintly, the sounds of ghosts weeping and wolves howling echoed from within.

"Brat, go on." Loris said faintly.

"Although being dispatched to Broken Sword Peak isn’t a good thing, you still have a chance to turn things around."

"One month from now, all newly entered disciples must participate in an assessment to determine the future focus and distribution of resources."

"Therefore, I hope you do not abandon yourself to despair. Under the premise of striving to stay alive, try your best to learn some skills."

"Many thanks, Elder Loris." Alex nodded, smiling faintly.

Loris brought him down from the sky and was just about to leave.

Alex stopped him. "Elder Loris, please wait a moment."

"Please accept this item, Elder."

Loris looked closely and saw Alex holding a pale green fruit in his hands. The fruit radiated abundant, pure azure light, as well as a faint, elegant fragrance and deep Zen intent.

Master Loris froze. "A Green Jade Immortal Fruit?!"

"You... how do you have this?!"

"This Green Jade Immortal Fruit is a High-Grade Heaven and Earth-tier spiritual herb. It is exceptionally nourishing and beneficial for a Mahayana Realm cultivator trying to break through to the Supreme Realm!" Loris said in astonishment.

"Even with a hundred thousand spirit stones, you might not be able to buy a single one!"
