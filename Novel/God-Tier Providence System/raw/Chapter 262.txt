Alex didn’t hate scheming and battling wits against powerhouses.

However, Knox was nothing more than an ant at the Nascent Soul Realm. Moreover, he possessed every single unique flaw of a protagonist without missing one: selfish, arrogant, and obstinate.

For this kind of trash, who had no real abilities yet loved to act tough and show off, Alex could normally slap him so hard his soul would scatter with a casual backhand.

But it just so happened that this guy possessed the protection of the Protagonist Halo and the death-exemption gold medal of Luck.

This forced Alex to not only restrain himself from killing him, but also to play all sorts of mind games with him.

Playing mind games with this kind of ant was like spending three days laying a trap just to catch a tiny fly.

Whether in the eyes of others or his own, Alex felt like an absolute idiot.

But fortunately, his various layouts had finally yielded remarkable results.

The Luck on Knox’s body had been reduced by more than half.

As long as he continued his efforts, it wouldn’t be long before he could turn him into a cripple, then slap him into trash with a single palm to relieve the hatred in his heart.

Once they had accumulated some qualifications and merits and became familiar with the Hale family’s situation, then he could consider letting them take on heavy responsibilities, such as Branch Saint Lords.

Returning to the Saint Lord’s Hall, Alex’s mood was immensely joyful. He temporarily disregarded resting and sleeping, simply opening the Providence Mall instead.

Right now, he held 400,000 Providence Points.

Although it was already a massive sum he had never reached before, it was stuck in an awkward range where it was too low for high-end items but too high for low-end ones.

He was currently at the peak of the Supreme Realm, a half-step Saint. Spiritual artifacts, secret manuals, and pills below the Supreme Realm were completely useless to him.

But using the Supreme Realm as the dividing line, the value of Saint-tier weapons, secret manuals, and pills above it all hovered around 500,000 Providence Points.

There were some relatively cheaper ones he could afford if he gritted his teeth, but they had absolutely nothing to do with the cultivation methods he practiced, making them of little significance.

Alex browsed for a long while but failed to find a desirable and suitable choice. Simply too lazy to think further about it, he chose to continue tackling the cultivation method in his hands, the Six Paths Wheel of Destruction Technique.
