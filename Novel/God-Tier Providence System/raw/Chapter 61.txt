Chapter 61

"As long as you can continuously and steadily progress..."

"...Then even if you empty out the entire Hale family vault, I will be happy!"

The crowd instantly revealed expressions of pure excitement. One by one, they rubbed their palms together, eager and ready to give it their all.

Julian secretly rubbed his hands.

"Saint Lord, what will the reward be for the next assembly?"

If the first place reward was another unconditional wish, then even if he had to risk his life, he absolutely had to seize the top spot.

"Good question." Alex smiled faintly.

"The next assembly will still distribute rewards based on the level of your progress."

"And the one who achieves the highest breakthrough will receive..."

"...The Seven Orifices Divine Heart, currently sealed within the Spirit Nurturing Case!"

"Holy shit!"

The entire crowd instantly boiled over.

The Seven Orifices Divine Heart symbolized the supreme, unparalleled grace the Saint Lord had once bestowed upon Isolde.

To refine this treasure, the Saint Lord had personally hunted down seven Mahayana Realm demonic beasts.

He used their demonic souls and blood essence to refine seven Strange Stones.

He then spent a staggering one million spirit stones to purchase various Heaven and Earth treasures.

Finally, the Four Great Elders joined forces to set up a massive formation, forging it continuously for forty nine days and nights.

It had nearly exhausted an entire year’s worth of the family’s resources just to bring this treasure into the world.

The sheer power of the Seven Orifices Divine Heart was enough to rival a Saint tier piritual treasure. If a cultivator below the Nascent Soul Realm obtained it, their cultivation could instantly skyrocket by three major realms.

If a cultivator above the Nascent Soul Realm obtained it, they could receive the guidance of the Heavenly Dao, making their future cultivation feel as though they had divine assistance.

Back then, the Saint Lord had smashed countless resources into Isolde. But her aptitude was simply far too mediocre.

She had eaten so many pills she had practically developed immunity, yet she was still stuck in the Foundation Establishment Realm.

It was precisely after obtaining the Seven Orifices Divine Heart that she skyrocketed straight to the Enlightenment Realm.

From then on, she basked in infinite glory within the family, throwing her weight around acting tyrannically.

Furthermore, the Seven Orifices Divine Heart contained the auspicious, protective power of the seven great beasts. When encountering danger, it could turn peril into safety and transform misfortune into blessings.

Whoever obtained this treasure would essentially be inheriting Isolde’s former position.

It could even be said they would become the absolute prime candidate to be the Hale family’s next Saint Lord.

In an instant, aside from Julian who felt slightly disappointed, the rest of the disciples were incomparably thrilled.

"However." Alex’s tone suddenly shifted, speaking coolly.

"If there is no progress after three months, you will still be punished."

"Within three months, whoever fails to break through even a single minor realm..."

"...will be punished by being sent out to execute one Grade B mission."

"The glory and rewards for completing the mission will belong entirely to the family."

"If you show zero progress for two consecutive assemblies..."

"...then the punishment will be executing two Grade B missions. And so on."

It was as if a basin of freezing cold water had been poured over the crowd.

"...A Grade B mission?"

"Isn’t... isn’t that a bit too difficult?"

Every major city in the Heavenly Flame Dynasty possessed a Mission Posting Pavilion.

Inside, all sorts of missions were hung up. Some posted by private citizens. Some by major clans. Some officially by the dynasty itself.

All missions were divided into six grades: A, B, C, D, E, and F. The higher the grade, the greater the spirit stone reward, but the harder it was to execute.

A Grade B mission basically required a cultivator at the Nascent Soul Realm or higher to complete it solo.

A hundred years ago, during the Hale family’s most desperate crisis, Alex had personally led three thousand disciples away from home for half a year. In that time, they executed over a thousand Grade A and Grade B missions.

They practically snatched up every single high level mission in the two surrounding cities, Azure Forest City and Falling Rain City. Only then did they save up enough spirit stones to help the family survive.

But after surviving that crisis, the major disciples essentially no longer needed to rely on life or death missions to make a living. A resource allowance of three hundred spirit stones a month was completely enough.

Why waste time and energy running around on dangerous missions? Wasn’t it better to stay safely within the holy land, cultivate diligently, and strive for a breakthrough?

But now, the Saint Lord had designated these missions as the punishment for the inspection assembly. Although somewhat better than outright deducting resources, it was still enough to fill many with dread.

"So that’s how it is..." Standing off to the side, Tobias stroked his beard and nodded with a satisfied smile.

"This move by the Saint Lord is truly brilliant."

"Using the format of an assembly and generous rewards to motivate the juniors, ensuring they never grow complacent and always strive to elevate their cultivation."

"And for those whose talent has dried up and can no longer progress..."

"He can send them out to execute missions and earn resources, which will then supply the other geniuses."

"The spirit stones they earn back can be used perfectly as rewards for those making progress..."

"Brilliant! Truly too brilliant!"

Tobias admired the Saint Lord’s wisdom so much he wanted to throw himself to the ground in worship. He believed this inspection assembly system could be passed down endlessly, allowing future generations to utilize it forever.

However, just as admiration surged endlessly in his heart like the waters of a great river, Alex suddenly turned his head to look at him.

"Tobias."

"Here!" Tobias hurriedly responded, taking a step forward.
