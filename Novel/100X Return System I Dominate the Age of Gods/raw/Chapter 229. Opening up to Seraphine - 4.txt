Chapter 229. Opening up to Seraphine - 4

"Are you telling me that you are alright about me marrying other women!!??" William asked with a flustered expression on his face.

His voice rose slightly in disbelief, his brows furrowed as he searched her face for any hint of teasing or hidden resentment. His grip on her shoulders tightened.

"Am I difficult to handle right now??" William asked with a visible frown. His pride took a small hit as he tilted his head, genuinely puzzled.

Sera just chuckled and looked at him with a gaze that only deepened his curiosity. There was something playful, something knowing in her eyes that made his heart beat a little faster.

Shaking his head, William could only sigh.

"Let’s go out; I have something to tell you," he said.

His tone turned more serious; the earlier confusion was now replaced with a resolve.

"Oh," Sera got curious about what he wanted to tell her; she followed his lead as William held her hands. Their fingers intertwined naturally, as if it were something they had done countless times before.

He brought her out and made her sit on the stairs of the hut.

The wooden steps were slightly warm from the sunlight, and a soft breeze carried the scent of the surrounding greenery.

"Sera, I don’t know how this sounds, but since you say that you are open about me marrying another woman, there is someone I want you to know about."

Sera just chuckled.

"I know, I am just messing with you!!" Her laughter lightened the mood instantly.

William gave her a look.

"Are you really sure about me taking in other women, or are you just testing me?? Well, I am already about to fail your test," he sighed.

There was a hint of vulnerability in his voice now. Sera looked at William with a pursed smile; she did not say anything and just looked at him, all restless and flustered.

She saw him getting on his knees in front of her and matched their eye level. He then clasped her hands, to her amusement.

The gesture was unexpectedly sincere.

"Sera, you will always remain my first love, and I will love you forever; even death won’t do us apart," he said and reiterated again.

Sera chuckled. "Tsk, who is this woman that is making you so restless, Willy? You know I am getting a bit jealous now."

"It’s alright; I can take it. No need to go out of your way and try to make me happy; I was never sad to begin with," Sera reassured William again.

Her gaze steadied him. William nodded and came to sit behind her, taking her in her arms.

He rubbed her hands and started telling her about Tamasya.

He told her about the first time he met Tamsya, how she became his master, and how her heart broke when he had told Tamsya about them and their first kiss.

"So you couldn’t keep our kiss a secret, huh?" Sera looked at William with squinted eyes.

A teasing glint returned to her expression.

William couldn’t help but pull her closer and hug her waist; he rested his head on her and whispered, "Thank you!!!"

Sera looked down at him; she caressed his head lightly and smiled subtly. The warmth between them deepened at this point.

"So when will I meet my junior sister?" Sera asked.

He picked Sera up in the air from her waist down.

"Oh, oii!! Stop, William!!"

"Hahaha!!" Sera giggled as she felt what he was doing; her hair fluttered with the wind.

Both of them looked at each other and giggled. A wave of nostalgia washed over them.

William often used to swing her this way when he used to meet her in Riverdale.

With time they had forgotten the innocence they once shared. But now, after the acceptance Sera had shown for William, he could not help but fall in love with her even more.

William locked his eyes on hers. The world around them seemed to fade for a moment.

Sera gulped with hesitation; her mind was churning whether she should let William or not.

Without waiting, William plunged; he kissed her cheeks, her neck, and her forehead before going for her soft lips.

Her eyes widened in shock, and she clasped her mouth. Her gaze landed on her nape, where ancient marks pulsed with faint green energy.

The marks glowed, reacting violently as if protecting something sacred.

She covered her loosened robes before running toward William, who was stunned by the impact.
