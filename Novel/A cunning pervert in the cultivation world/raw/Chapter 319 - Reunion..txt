Chapter 319: Reunion.

Jian Ruyi was startled by the sudden attack but she reacted instantly, drawing her sword and slashing toward the invisible blade.

Ting!

Sparks flew as steel met steel and a sharp gust of wind burst outward as Jian Ruyi managed to block the strike.

But instead of relief, anger flashed across her face as she turned toward Liu Yanyue.

"What are you doing?!" she shouted at Liu Yanyue who just stood there and frozen, as if she had seen a ghost.

Jian Ruyi’s expression darkened at the sight.

Someone at Liu Yanyue’s level should have been able to handle that attack easily as it felt more like a greeting than a killing strike.

But Liu Yanyue didn’t seem to hear her at all.

Slowly, she turned her head toward the direction the attack had come from.

"Senior brother...?" she murmured, her voice trembling with disbelief.

From the distance, Yan Moxuan stepped forward with a bright smile spreading across his face.

It was probably the first time he had ever smiled so openly and happily since arriving here.

"Haha! I see junior sister still remembers this useless senior brother of yours!"

His eyes were already tinged red, burning with emotion as he clapped his hands lightly.

"And I heard you became the strongest outer sect disciple? I must congratulate you... you’re finally one step closer to your dream."

Liu Yanyue remained silent, her mind in turmoil as countless thoughts raced through her.

After a long moment, she spoke softly,

"You’re still alive..."

"Oh? I’m not only alive... but stronger too!"

Yan Moxuan laughed and then releasing a sharp, crimson aura that pulsed around him.

But Liu Yanyue’s expression only grew more shaken as she sensed the demonic qi within it.

"...You became a demonic disciple?"

Yan Moxuan sneered.

"Demonic? To me, all paths are the same... they are all merely a stepping stones to my strength."

He glanced around at the surroundings with disdain before turning back to her.

Then, reaching out his hand, his expression softened into a gentle smile.

"How about we find somewhere quiet for our reunion?"

Liu Yanyue fell silent.

Her heart was filled with questions... yet, for some reason, she found herself wanting to take that hand... the hand of the kind senior brother and ask him what had happened.

But then—

"Don’t listen to that trash words!"

Jian Ruyi shouted as she suddenly appeared in front of Yan Moxuan, her sword already slashing toward his chest.

ZING!

A radiant arc of silver flashed through the air.

CLANG!

Sparks burst forth as her blade halted midair, as if it had struck something invisible.

"Oh?" Yan Moxuan smiled faintly at her. "So you’re still alive, little girl."

His gaze turned cold, a hint of mockery in his voice.

"And it seems you’ve come to lose that newly gained life of yours again... how foolish."

Jian Ruyi’s expression remained icy.

Without wasting another word, she launched into a flurry of attacks.

Clang!

Clang!

Clang!

Each strike collided with an unseen barrier, sending sparks flying as waves of sharp sword intent tore through the air like invisible blades.

From a distance, it looked almost bizarre.... like Jian Ruyi was both attacking and dodging on her own, as if performing a deadly sword dance, while Yan Moxuan simply stood there without moving.

None of the surrounding disciples dared to approach that invisible storm of blades.

The sheer pressure made it feel as though they would be cut down instantly if they stepped any closer.

Jian Ruyi’s eyes narrowed.

Every strike was being stopped... blocked by something unseen, like an invisible force that distorted the air around him.

This wasn’t her first time fighting him... and she knew just how troublesome he was.

"Tch...!"

She slid back, withdrawing as the ground beneath her feet cracked from the force she had unleashed.

Only then did Yan Moxuan tilt his head, his lips curling into a faint, amused smile.

"Finished?"

His voice was soft... almost disappointed.

Jian Ruyi’s grip tightened around her sword.

Behind her, Liu Yanyue still hadn’t moved.

"Liu Yanyue!" Jian Ruyi snapped, her voice cold and sharp. "Wake up! That thing is no longer your senior brother!"

Yan Moxuan chuckled at her words.

"That thing?"

He let out a low laugh, shaking his head as if amused by a child’s ignorance.

"And yet you lost so pathetically to that thing... don’t you remember?"

Then—

His aura surged, his eyes glowing with a menacing light.

"...Or do you desperately need to be reminded again?"

A deep crimson energy burst outward, thick with demonic qi, pressing down on everything like a suffocating tide.

The air grew heavy... oppressive.

Even the ground beneath them seemed to groan.

The surrounding crowd swallowed nervously under the crushing pressure.

Yue Lan and the others frowned, already preparing to step in and support Jian Ruyi.

But then—

BOOM!

BOOM!

Chains burst from the ground beneath Yue Lan’s feet, shooting upward as they tried to bind her.

Yet Yue Lan remained calm.

With a single swing of her sword, an icy wave of cold qi exploded outward, freezing the chains midair.

"Haha, we meet again, oh beautiful fairy!"

A casual voice echoed from the side as another handsome demonic disciple stepped forward, his presence just as imposing.

Yue Lan frowned when she saw that Ye Mo was still alive.

’Strange... didn’t Li Feng say he had already killed him?’

She knew Li Feng had no reason to lie.

And yet... this man had survived being killed not once, but twice... by both her and Li Feng.

There had to be some kind of technique at play.

Yue Lan’s gaze turned cold.

"Then I’ll just kill him again..."

She refused to believe there was any heaven-defying technique that could be used endlessly.

There had to be a limit.

Seeing Yue Lan being targeted, Ning Jianli and the others immediately drew their swords, preparing to step in—

But then, a talisman shot high into the air, burning as it rose before bursting apart into a rain of fireballs.

Seeing this, Ning Jianli shouted.

"Dodge!"

The righteous disciples scattered, activating their movement techniques.

BOOM!

BOOM!

BOOM!

Flames engulfed the battlefield as explosions rained down one after another.

"Hahaha~! Finally! I was starting to doubt myself, but it seems... I’m still amazing!"
