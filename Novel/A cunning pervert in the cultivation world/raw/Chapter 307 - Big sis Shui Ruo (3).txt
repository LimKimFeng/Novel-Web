Li Feng blinked and repeated,

"You want... wine?"

Shui Ruo hurriedly added, "I want good wine! High-quality wine!"

Her beautiful blue eyes seemed to sparkle at the mere mention of wine, bright and expectant like a child who had just remembered her favorite treat after a long, tiring day.

Li Feng blinked again and fell silent.

It wasn’t that he didn’t want to give her wine... he simply didn’t have any at the moment.

But then he suddenly remembered something.

In the system, outside the main powerful talents like Alchemist or Formation Master, there were many smaller, niche paths.

Among them, he remembered one particularly unique talent: Wine Master.

Li Feng’s mind raced as the system interface flickered in his sea of consciousness as he check the details.

The Wine Master talent wasn’t one of those flashy, combat-oriented professions that dominated the cultivation world.... no explosive pill-refining like Alchemist, no world-shaking arrays like Formation Master but it had its own quiet, intoxicating allure.

Some spiritual wines could cleanse meridians, boost qi purity, induce faint enlightenment, or even help renew and balance yang or yin energy...

In some ways, they were almost similar to the pills refined by alchemists.

The key difference, however, was that alchemist pills were far more powerful, offered greater variety, and could be produced in mere hours or days, whereas spiritual wines often required years... or even decades of careful fermentation and aging to reach their full potency and truly aid one’s cultivation.

Because of that, very few cultivators bothered walking this path.

Most saw it as slow, inefficient, and impractical.

Only a handful of eccentric old monsters who had already reached the peak of their realm, grown bored with endless closed-door cultivation, and had nothing better to do would patiently tinker with vintages for centuries, treating brewing like an art form rather than a tool for power.

Li Feng, however, was not like most cultivators.

Right now, with Shui Ruo’s sparkling blue eyes locked on him, full of expectation and childlike excitement.

He made up his mind.

’I’m currently rich in favor points anyway. I should buy this talent to soothe this amazing big sister...’

A small, confident smile curved Li Feng’s lips.

Without hesitation, he immediately purchased the Wine Master talent and upgraded it straight to Rank 2.

Then,

Countless streams of information flooded his mind in an instant.

Knowledge of spiritual grape varieties, intricate fermentation arrays, aging techniques under different moon phases, rare spirit ingredients, complex aroma profiles, flavor layering, and more.

He sorted through it all with practiced ease, already calculating exactly how to brew a high-quality wine on the spot.

But Shui Ruo started to panic a little when she saw him go completely silent.

’D-Did I ask too much?’

Deep down, she really didn’t blame Li Feng that much.

She had only been teasing him a bit, half-hoping he might actually have some wine hidden away in a storage ring or something.

She hadn’t tasted a single drop, even before entering the secret realm because her master had strictly forbidden it, claiming it would "cloud her dao heart" and "dull her spirit."

And now, after narrowly surviving a crisis... and after experiencing all that overwhelming pleasure... she suddenly craved a drink more than anything.

Just one sip to calm her fluttering heart and aching body.

But seeing him so quiet, Shui Ruo couldn’t help but feel a twinge of worry creep in.

She glanced around, then remembered his earlier words.

Clearing her throat lightly to grab his attention, she open her mouth,

"Ahem... How about this?" she said, her voice a perfect mix of shyness and bold mischief.

"In exchange for the wine, I’ll even help you with your yang overflowing problem!"

Li Feng’s eyes snapped back into focus instantly.

"...Help me?"

"Yes! Just come to me whenever your yang needs to be released!"

Not to mention this would let her repay him at least somewhat for saving her life.
