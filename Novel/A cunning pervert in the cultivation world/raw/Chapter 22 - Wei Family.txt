Chapter 22: Wei Family

Li Feng’s curiosity piqued as he caught sight of the store’s name above in elegant characters: "Wei Alchemy Hall."

The dark ink had faded slightly, the wooden plaque weathered by years of sun and rain. It wasn’t shabby, just seemed a bit worn down. Compared to the lively pill shops nearby, this one looked oddly quiet. A pair of customers exited with dark expressions, muttering complaints.

"Hmm," Li Feng murmured, tilting his head. "That hall seems... deserted. For an alchemy shop in a city like this, that’s strange."

Lin Yu followed his gaze and gave a small sigh. "That’s Wei Alchemy Hall. It belongs to the Wei family. They were once a rising star among the local clans in this city, almost strong enough to become the fifth major family of Cloudveil City."

Li Feng ask back "Almost?"

"Yes." Lin Yu’s expression turned pity. "Three years ago, their patriarch died during a breakthrough to Middle stage of Foundation Establishment Realm. At least, that’s the official story. Some say it a backlash from heart demon tribulation. Others say he was being poison. Then, as if fate wasn’t cruel enough, their old ancestor, the only Late Stage Foundation Realm expert in their line, passed away soon after in secluded cultivation."

"Both at once?" Li Feng’s tone was casual, but his mind was already suspicious of this coincidence.

Lin Yu nodded. "Without those two pillars, their influence crumbled. Rivals started circling like wolves. Their trade routes were taken, allies vanished. And their greatest asset, the alchemy hall has been dying slowly."

Li Feng’s eyes drifted back to the faded plaque and empty counters inside. "What happened?"

"Their master alchemist is also very old and bedridden," Lin Yu explained. "No one else in the clan matches his skill. Now they can’t produce high-grade pills anymore. And if they want to buy from the sect or other shops, the cost is too high to make worthwhile profit."

"Hmm..." Li Feng let out a low hum, eyes still on the faded plaque, then an idea started forming in his mind. This... maybe I can try to sell my pills to this family. I can probably get some bigger share during negotiation using their situation as my advantage, but the risk is...

Lin Yu continued as they walked slowly past. "You should know, Cloudveil City is dominated by four major families. The Ye family who controls artifact forging, they produce some of the finest weapons in the surrounding area. The Peng family deals in alchemy and medicinal trade; they’ve had a monopoly for decades. The Hong family specializes in talismans and array work, while the Zhang family handles spirit beast breeding and trade."

"And the Wei family was going to join them?" Li Feng asked.

Li Feng isn’t dumb. The fall of the Wei family probably has something to do with the four major families here.

"Yes. Before tragedy struck, the Wei family was powerful in both strength and commerce. People thought they would rise to sit alongside the four. Now..." Lin Yu shook his head. "Now they’re barely holding on. The sect won’t intervene, of course. So long as taxes are paid and peace is kept, the sect doesn’t care who rises or falls."

Li Feng then asked, "So if the Wei family collapses, no one will step in?"

"Why would the Sect care?" Lin Yu’s tone turn bitterly amused. "This is the cultivation world, Junior Brother. The strong take everything, while the weak vanish. Of course, it all has to be within the Sect rules."

Which means... the Wei family situation is probably more dire than I thought. Li Feng’s thoughts stirred like ripples on a pond, but his face remained calm, still weighing whether it was worth the risk to work with the Wei family.

Suddenly, the chatter around them softened. A sleek carriage rolled to a stop in front of the Wei Alchemy Hall, its polished wood gleaming with the family crest. Attendants hurried to lower the steps.

Li Feng paused, looking curiously. He expected a steward, maybe a young heir. Instead...

A mature figure descended.

Her black hair was tied up in a high, elegant bun, pinned with jade hair sticks. One loose strand fell against her cheek and slid down the side of her neck, soft and out of place against her sharp, commanding look.

Wei Meilin paused at the entrance to the hall, exchanging a few words with the anxious clerk. Her voice carried across the street, calm and melodic, yet edged with authority. But beneath that, a hint of fatigue lingered, barely noticeable.

"Send word to the steward. The next shipment must arrive on time. No more excuses."

The clerk bowed repeatedly. "Yes, Madam Wei!"

Li Feng’s gaze followed her every movement, especially the slow, deliberate sway of her full, round hips beneath the deep purple silk. Just as Li Feng seemed stuck in thought, he glanced down, and found his little brother already trying to voice its opinion.

Li Feng sighed inwardly. "...Alright, anything for you brother." His lips curved into a slow, dangerous grin. Heh so what if the four families don’t like it? He leaned closer, his voice dropping to a low, serious tone. "I’m telling you this for your own good, junior brother. Mortal entanglements like this? They look sweet at first, but they’ll drag you down before you even notice. One moment you’re chasing a woman, the next you’re years behind in cultivation."

When Li Feng heard this, he couldn’t help but give Lin Yu a strange look.

Lin Yu didn’t seem to notice Li Feng’s gaze. Instead, he glanced toward a bustling street corner where a crowd was gathering. "There’s a gathering nearby. It’s a good chance to observe the sect’s influence in the city and learn more about the families here. You should come with me."

Li Feng slowly shook his head, his voice steady and confident. "Senior Brother, go ahead. I want to have a private talk with Madam Wei a bit regarding alchemy."

Lin Yu, who seemed frustrated with this stubborn junior, couldn’t help but walk away in annoyance.

With that, he slipped into the crowd, leaving Li Feng alone with his thoughts and the enticing presence of the Wei matriarch. Then, with a grin, Li Feng walked into the store.
